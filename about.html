Support

Scholarships fund up to 48 months of full-time doctoral study with a maintenance award at UKRI base levels and include Â£10,000 to support individual research and training needs. 5 scholarships are available each year.

Timeline

We are currently recruiting for entry into the programme in October 2024. The deadline for applications is the 22nd March 2024. Successful applicants will be notified on the 19th April 2024.

International scholarships

Over the course of the programme 3 scholarships may be offered to international students. An additional 3 scholarships are available to students from East African Community states.

Masters scholarships

To promote the progression of Black students and students from low income backgrounds, up to 3 scholarships are available over the course of the programme to fund students to undertake a master's degree before progressing to a PhD. These scholarships offer 5 years of funding, including fees and a maintenance award for all years. In the first year, students will undertake an MSc in Data Analytics or an MSc in Quantitative Methods in Biodiversity, Conservation & Epidemiology, before progressing to the PhD programme.
